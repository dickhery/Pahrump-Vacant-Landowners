Potential Solutions

As vacant land owners in Pahrump, Nevada, we face significant challenges due to Village Residential (VR) zoning requirements and water rights restrictions under State Engineer Order #1293A. Below, we outline factual, practical solutions based on Nye County Code Title 17, Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS), and ongoing advocacy efforts. These options aim to restore your property rights, allowing use and enjoyment as afforded by the U.S. Constitution (Fifth Amendment) and Nevada Constitution (Article 1, Section 1). Always consult legal or planning professionals for personalized advice.

1. Advocacy and Petitioning the Nye County Commission

Lobby the Nye County Board of Commissioners to amend zoning ordinances for compliance with constitutional protections. Current VR zoning (Nye County Code 17.04.240) mandates public utilities for residential development, often unavailable in rural areas, rendering lots "zombie lots." Propose changes like "tiny home" zoning or "Super Conservation Lots" to allow off-grid living, rainwater harvesting, or bulk water delivery. The Pahrump Vacant Landowners Group has presented at commission meetings (e.g., May 14, 2025), emphasizing property taxes paid without infrastructure benefits. Attend public hearings or submit petitions via the Nye County website to push for revisions that align with NRS 278.010 (promoting public welfare through zoning).

2. Acquiring and Relinquishing Water Rights for Well Permits

To drill a domestic well in Basin #162, relinquish 2 acre-feet (AF) of existing water rights annually per Order #1293A (amended 2018). Water rights are property under NRS 533.005 and can be purchased through brokers or the Nevada Division of Water Resources database (average cost: $5,000–$10,000 per AF as of 2025). Contact a water rights surveyor (e.g., Civilwise Services) to identify and transfer rights. Note: No guarantee of well success, and failed wells offer no recourse (NRS 534.120). This complies with state law but may be challenged as a taking (Fifth Amendment; see McCarran v. Sisolak, 2006).

3. Funding Infrastructure Extensions

Privately fund extensions of city water/sewer lines through Pahrump Utility Company Inc. (PUCI) or Great Basin Water Co. Costs range from $50,000–$200,000 per parcel, depending on distance (Nye County Code 16.28 requires developer-funded improvements for subdivisions). Apply for a development agreement with the county planning department. While expensive, this fulfills VR zoning utility requirements (Nye County Code 17.04.240). Property taxes (average $100–$300/year per acre) should theoretically fund such infrastructure, but delays persist; advocate for county subsidies.

4. Applying for Zoning Variances or Rezoning

Seek a variance (Nye County Code 17.04.860) or rezoning (e.g., to Rural Estates or Mixed Use) through the Pahrump Regional Planning Commission. Demonstrate hardship (e.g., no utilities) and public benefit. Fees: $500–$1,000; process includes public hearings. Successful examples include rezoning from VR-20 to RH-4.5 (March 9, 2022). This allows alternative uses like off-grid systems if rezoned appropriately.

5. Legal Action and Class Action Lawsuit

After exhausting administrative options, pursue litigation against Nye County or the state for regulatory takings (Lucas v. South Carolina, 1992; City of Las Vegas v. 180 Land Co., 2024). Claims include violations of equal protection (14th Amendment) and due process. Our group is exploring a class action; fill out the interest form in the contact section. Consult attorneys specializing in land use (e.g., Nevada Real Estate Law firms).

6. Selling or Leasing the Land

Market the lot for non-residential uses or to buyers aware of restrictions. Average VR-zoned lot price: $5,000–$15,000 (LandWatch, 2025). Lease for solar farms, storage, or recreation (permitted under conditional uses, Nye County Code 17.04.240.E). This recoups investment without development.

7. Off-Grid and Alternative Systems (Where Allowed)

If rezoned or variance granted, install solar power, composting toilets, or rainwater systems (NRS 278.580 allows sustainable practices). Bulk water delivery is prohibited in some codes but could be advocated for change.

8. Community and State-Level Advocacy

Join efforts for interbasin water transfers (Nye County Water District) or legislative changes (e.g., AB104/SB36, 2025, for rights retirement). Participate in groundwater management plans (Pahrump Basin #162 Plan, 2016) to address over-appropriation (59,000 AF committed vs. 12,000 AF yield).

These solutions empower owners to reclaim rights. Join our Facebook group for updates and collective action.
